About Me

As a committed problem solver and an independent introvert, I am someone rather artistically inclined enough to say these elements get translated well into my work.

My introduction to code was during my days in University writting my first Hello world! in Java. To be honest, I had a rough time trying to grasp programming concepts. I came to the realization that, I am naturally wired to question everything often finding myself putting together questions like: Who started this idea? What problem is this meant to solve? Where can I apply this skill? When should I apply this skill? Why do I need to understand this? How can I explain this in layman's terms?

You see, once you figure out your personal method of learning, everything else begins to make sense.

Technology

Among my interests for technology you may find me reading about System Design, Engineering Principles and Tools, to Frontend and Backend concepts.

Lately I have been looking into the newest trends with Frontend tools and improving my skills, because as you know, technology is constantly evolving and we must keep up with the trend.

Currently, I am reading about HTML semantics, and practicing my CSS. While studying Front End tech and tools, my favorite library by far has been React.js. Nothing made me happier than when 'hooks' were released.

I whole heartedly shine when my work revolves around developing features to create fulfilling user experience. My intuition always steers towards disciplined software engineering practices like automated testing, code reviews, and writing beautiful, readable code. In the end, simple is closest to perfection. Although I have a sincere commitment to crafting readable and dependable code, there is still a lot I need to learn.

As a Software Engineer, it is immensely important to always keep yourself humble about the knows and don't knows (i.e. keeping your ego in check). Why? you say. Because a Software Engineer is meant to work in a team environment. Your team will always see things you may not see on your own work and it is essential to both be able to give and receive constructive criticism and direction when needed.

I may candidly say that I have a good understanding of, and practical experience with, web application development to build a single page application. This page, for example, is built using semantic HTML and CSS. It can and could have been built in React, but I just needed to display content and make it look pretty. I found a color palette I liked and applied it. It is quite possible that the more content I add to this page, I may decide later on to convert to a modern library like React.js.

Other than HTML and CSS which are the heart of Front End in web application development, I have exposure to many other tools and technologies. My use of various of these technologies has been in production including internal and external applications. You may also find some of my public repositories available on my GitHub.
