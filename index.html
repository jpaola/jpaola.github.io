About Me

I've always been a committed problem solver, an independent introvert, and someone rather artistically inclined. As a software engineer it's safe to say that all of these elements get translated well into my work.

I started learning to code when I was in University. The concepts did not come easy at first. Though after a rough time trying to grasp programming concepts, I realized the reason of my struggle to pick them up. I am naturally wired to question everything often finding myself putting together questions like: Who started this idea? What problem is this meant to solve? Where can I apply this skill? When should I apply this skill? Why do I need to understand this? How can I explain this in layman's terms? You see, once you figure out your personal method of learning, everything else begins to make sense.

Nowadays I am constantly learning new things from technology, to art, to literature, to music, to personal health and fitness. Among my interests for technology you may find me reading about System Design, Engineering Principles and Tools, to Frontend and Backend concepts. Lately I have been looking into the newest trends with Frontend tools like HTML semantics, CSS, JavaScript, React.js.

Art
